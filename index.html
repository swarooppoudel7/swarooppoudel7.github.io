Influence of Messaging Styles and personality on charitable giving attitude

Over two-thirds of all donations to charities in the US come from individuals. Charitable organizations rely heavily on individuals to make donations for their prosperity. These Individuals make these donation decisions based on internal factors like their personality and beliefs or external factors like the amount of money they have or persuasion. Understanding these factors can increase community support and promote pro-social behavior. I investigated the influence of personality, messaging, and the amount of fictitious money someone has on people's donation attitudes. Participants on Amazon MTurk filled out a personality index, then declared how much they would donate to a charity based on a poster they observed. There was no main effect of messaging or fictitious amount on donation amount, and there was no interaction between them. However, we observed that individuals high in extraversion had small but significantly higher levels of giving.. Find Poster Here

Relationship Between Technology Access and Subjective Well-Being: An Analysis of Low-Middle Income Nations

Rising nation-level wealth is associated with increased subjective well-being (SWB). This paper looks at the complementary idea that access to information and communication technologies (ICT) has the potential to improve SWB. Increases in ICT access can empower people with lower levels of wealth, as it may provide novel ways to complete day-to-day tasks more efficiently and safely. However, the same ICTs also have the potential to affect people who overuse them negatively. This can be seen in technological interference with relationships and routines and increased levels of distress for heavy technology users. This is especially true in technologically saturated places, such as many modern, Western societies. This study examines the ICT access-SWB relationship, exploring wealth as a potential moderator. Using data collected by UNICEF, this research explores these relationships in 24 low- to middle-wealth nations, controlling for other relevant factors. The data (N = ) show that ICT use is most strongly predicts SWB among people who are the least wealthy and that the strength of this relationship decreases with increases in wealth. Find Poster Here

Impacts of internal migration on well-being in Bangladesh

This research investigated the impact of internal migration on the well-being of the migrant population in Bangladesh. Specifically, the research empirically tests the association between internal migration and three different measures of well-being: subjective well-being, material well-being, and happiness. Mass migration has been a prominent aspect of the century, with the massive rise in the global urban population. Major economic influences have been researched extensively due to this mass migration. However, psychological aspects of internal migration on migrants have been analyzed to a much lesser extent in economic literature. The data suggested that an increased level of technology access and a higher level of education was the largest predictor of the well-being of women regardless of migration status. Suggesting policy implications that promote increased access to technology and wider education campaigns. Find presentation here

Where to eat: Logo color and tones of establishments and likelyhood of visiting

Logos of products, brands, and restaurants may attract consumers based on the emphasis consumers place on the visual appearance of their logo design. The color tones of logos may also attract consumers based on their prior association with the particular color. Our research looked specifically at how the color tones of restaurant logos would influence the likelihood of someone visiting. Eighty-nine participants completed a survey that asked them to rate their likelihood of visiting a restaurant based solely on the given logo. The results were consistent with past research about the color being important to consumers, but consumers did not prefer a specific hue for visiting resturants. These findings can allow restaurants and businesses to be more proactive when choosing or designing their logo.
